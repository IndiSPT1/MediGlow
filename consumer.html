Health Risks from Unsafe Food

Urban consumers may suffer foodborne illnesses from:

Contaminated or unhygienic preparation
Improper food storage in city markets
Bacteria, viruses, or parasites

Symptoms include stomach pain, diarrhea, vomiting, and long-term complications.

Chemical and Artificial Contamination

Harmful substances include:

Excessive preservatives/additives
Pesticide residues in produce
Unsafe coloring agents

Long-term exposure increases risk of chronic diseases in urban populations.

Expired or Poor-Quality Food

Expired packaged foods still sold in city shops
Poor quality processed food in urban markets
Misleading labels hiding expiration dates

This increases the chance of food poisoning outbreaks.

Unsafe Packaging in Urban Areas

More fast food and sugary products available
Healthy eating habits decline city-wide
Risk of obesity and chronic diseases rises

Overall Urban Consumer Impact

Unsafe food affects both health and city society.

Higher health costsIncreased medical expenses for city residents
Lower productivityIllness reduces daily workforce performance
Reduced quality of lifeEspecially in low-income urban communities

Urban Food Environments

Where unsafe food practices are most frequently seen.

School canteensLow hygiene preparation in city schools
Market foodsExpired or low-quality packaged goods
Open stallsExposure to dust, flies, urban pollution
